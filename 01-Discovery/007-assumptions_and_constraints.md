# Assumptions and Constraints
 
**Document Type:** Project Assumptions and Constraints  
**Classification:** Portfolio Case Study – Fictional Organisation  
**Version:** 1.0  

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the assumptions and constraints used when developing the Project SentinelCare cybersecurity governance, risk, and compliance assessment.

The assumptions provide a realistic foundation for the assessment while recognising that the organisation, systems, and security environment are fictional and created for portfolio purposes.


# 2. General Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-001 | Westbridge University Hospitals Foundation Trust is a fictional NHS organisation created for cybersecurity portfolio demonstration purposes. |
| A-002 | The Trust operates within a similar regulatory environment to NHS healthcare providers in England. |
| A-003 | The Trust has an established IT function responsible for supporting clinical and corporate technology services. |
| A-004 | Cybersecurity is managed as part of the Trust's wider information governance and enterprise risk management approach. |
| A-005 | The Trust Board recognises cybersecurity as a strategic and patient safety concern. |
| A-006 | The information provided in this case study represents a realistic scenario rather than an assessment of an actual NHS organisation. |


# 3. Technology Environment Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-007 | The Trust operates a hybrid technology environment combining on-premises infrastructure and cloud services. |
| A-008 | Microsoft Azure is used to host selected workloads and digital services. |
| A-009 | Microsoft 365 is used for corporate productivity, collaboration, and communication services. |
| A-010 | Clinical systems integrate with multiple internal and external healthcare platforms. |
| A-011 | Medical devices are connected to clinical networks and require security management. |
| A-012 | Legacy technology exists due to the extended operational lifespan of healthcare systems and medical equipment. |
| A-013 | System ownership information is incomplete and requires improvement through asset management activities. |



# 4. Data and Information Governance Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-014 | The Trust processes highly sensitive information including patient, staff, and research data. |
| A-015 | Patient information is classified as confidential under UK GDPR requirements. |
| A-016 | Information assets require defined ownership and accountability. |
| A-017 | Data classification practices are inconsistent and require standardisation. |
| A-018 | Some information may exist outside approved storage locations due to historical working practices. |
| A-019 | Information sharing occurs with NHS partners, suppliers, and healthcare providers. |



# 5. Cybersecurity Governance Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-020 | The Trust has a Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) responsible for cybersecurity governance. |
| A-021 | Cybersecurity policies exist but require review, alignment, and formal governance approval. |
| A-022 | Cyber risk management processes are immature and require formalisation. |
| A-023 | Cyber risks are not consistently recorded, assessed, or reported across the organisation. |
| A-024 | Security responsibilities exist but require clearer ownership and accountability. |
| A-025 | The Trust requires improved executive-level cybersecurity reporting. |


# 6. Identity and Access Management Assumptions


| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-026 | Users require access to multiple clinical and corporate systems. |
| A-027 | Access permissions may have accumulated over time without regular review. |
| A-028 | Privileged accounts require additional governance and monitoring. |
| A-029 | Multi-factor authentication adoption is incomplete across all services. |
| A-030 | Identity governance improvements are required to support least privilege access. |



# 7. Supplier and Third-Party Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-031 | The Trust depends on more than 150 third-party suppliers. |
| A-032 | Suppliers may have varying levels of cybersecurity maturity. |
| A-033 | Supplier security requirements are not consistently applied across contracts. |
| A-034 | Some suppliers require access to Trust systems or sensitive information. |
| A-035 | A formal supplier security assurance process is required. |


# 8. Risk Assessment Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-036 | Cyber risks are assessed using a likelihood and impact-based scoring methodology. |
| A-037 | Risk ratings represent a point-in-time assessment and may change as controls improve. |
| A-038 | Business owners are responsible for accepting and managing risks associated with their services. |
| A-039 | The CISO provides cybersecurity risk guidance and oversight. |
| A-040 | Risk treatment decisions consider patient safety, regulatory requirements, and operational impact. |



# 9. Compliance Assessment Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-041 | The Trust is expected to align with NHS cybersecurity and information governance requirements. |
| A-042 | Assessments are performed against recognised frameworks including NCSC CAF, ISO/IEC 27001, and NHS DSP Toolkit. |
| A-043 | Compliance gaps represent improvement opportunities rather than confirmed regulatory failures. |
| A-044 | Evidence availability may vary depending on the maturity of existing processes. |
| A-045 | Framework mappings are performed at a high level for portfolio demonstration purposes. |
| A-046 | As an acute NHS Trust, WUHNFT is assumed to be designated an Operator of Essential Services (OES) under the NIS Regulations 2018, for which the NCSC CAF is the relevant assessment methodology. |



# 10. Incident Management Assumptions

| ID | Assumption |
|---|---|
| A-047 | The Trust maintains an incident response capability. |
| A-048 | Incident response procedures exist but require testing and improvement. |
| A-049 | Cyber incidents may require coordination between technical, clinical, legal, and communication teams. |
| A-050 | Major incidents may require notification to regulators and external stakeholders. |
| A-051 | Lessons learned activities are used to improve future resilience. |



# 11. Constraints

## 11.1 Portfolio Constraints

| ID | Constraint |
|---|---|
| C-001 | The organisation and environment are fictional and do not represent an actual NHS Trust. |
| C-002 | Detailed system configurations and operational data are simulated. |
| C-003 | Assessment findings are based on the defined scenario rather than real audit evidence. |



## 11.2 Resource Constraints

| ID | Constraint |
|---|---|
| C-004 | Security improvement activities must consider healthcare operational pressures. |
| C-005 | Legacy clinical technology may limit the ability to implement immediate security improvements. |
| C-006 | Budget and resource availability may influence implementation timelines. |



## 11.3 Assessment Constraints

| ID | Constraint |
|---|---|
| C-007 | The assessment focuses on governance, risk, and compliance rather than technical penetration testing. |
| C-008 | Security controls are assessed conceptually rather than through live technical validation. |
| C-009 | Evidence samples are assumed for demonstration purposes. |
| C-010 | The assessment does not replace a formal NHS DSP Toolkit submission or ISO certification audit. |
| C-011 | The assessment aligns to the NHS DSP Toolkit's evidence structure to support future submission readiness but does not itself constitute a DSPT submission (see [005-project_scope.md](005-project_scope.md)). |



# 12. Assessment Principles

Project SentinelCare follows these principles:

- Risk-based decision making.
- Protection of patient safety.
- Security by design.
- Least privilege access.
- Defence in depth.
- Continuous improvement.
- Evidence-based assurance.
- Clear ownership and accountability.


# 13. Conclusion

These assumptions and constraints provide the foundation for the Project SentinelCare assessment.

They ensure that the cybersecurity improvement programme remains realistic, structured, and aligned with the responsibilities expected of a Cyber Security GRC Analyst working within a healthcare environment.
