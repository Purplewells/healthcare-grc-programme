# Information Security Policy

**Organisation:** Westbridge Hospitals Trust (WHT)
**Document Type:** Policy
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
**Classification:** Portfolio Case Study – Fictional Organisation
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-07-23
**Review Date:** 2027-07-23

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out WHT's overarching requirements for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information and information systems. It establishes the baseline control expectations that all department-, system-, and domain-specific policies (including [054-access_control_policy](054-access_control_policy.md) and [055-supplier_security_policy](055-supplier_security_policy.md)) must satisfy at minimum.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all WHT staff, contractors, students, researchers, and third parties who access, process, or store Trust information or use Trust information systems, across all clinical, corporate, and digital functions described in [../../01-Discovery/005-project_scope](../../01-Discovery/005-project_scope.md).

## 3. Policy Statements

1. Information security is governed centrally through the Cyber Security Governance Group (CSGG), chartered in [../051-security_strategy](../051-security_strategy.md) §4, which reviews policy application and control effectiveness on a monthly cycle.
2. All information assets must have an assigned owner, consistent with the master asset register ([../../02-Asset-Management/022-master_assets_register.xlsx](../../02-Asset-Management/022-master_assets_register.xlsx)), who is accountable for its protection.
3. Information must be classified and handled in line with [../../06-Information-Governance/041-data_classification](../../06-Information-Governance/041-data_classification.md); uncontrolled copies of classified information outside approved systems are prohibited.
4. Access to information systems must follow the principle of least privilege and be governed by [054-access_control_policy](054-access_control_policy.md).
5. All cyber risks must be identified, assessed, and treated in accordance with the authoritative methodology in [../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology](../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology.md), and recorded in the central [risk_register](../../04-Risk-Management/risk_register.md).
6. Security incidents must be reported immediately to the Cyber Security Team and managed under the Trust's incident management process (see [../../08-Incident-Management/](../../08-Incident-Management/)).
7. Third-party access to Trust information or systems is subject to [055-supplier_security_policy](055-supplier_security_policy.md).
8. Medical devices and connected clinical equipment are subject to the asset and risk controls described in [../../04-Risk-Management/042-medical_device_risk](../../04-Risk-Management/042-medical_device_risk.md), including compensating controls where vendor patching is unavailable.
9. Compliance with applicable frameworks (NCSC CAF, ISO/IEC 27001:2022, NHS DSPT) must be maintained and re-assessed at least annually, as tracked in [../../03-Current-State-Assessment/](../../03-Current-State-Assessment/).
10. All staff must complete security awareness training, with completion rates reported to the CSGG.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

Roles and accountabilities for this policy are defined in [../052-roles_and_responsibilities](../052-roles_and_responsibilities.md). In summary: the CISO owns this policy and its enforcement; Information Asset Owners are accountable for the assets under their ownership; the CSGG reviews policy application and effectiveness on behalf of the Trust Board via the Audit and Risk Committee.

## 5. Compliance, Exceptions & Enforcement

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action for staff, or contract review for third parties, in line with the Trust's HR and procurement policies. Exceptions must be formally requested, risk-assessed against [../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology](../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology.md), and approved by the CISO or the CSGG where the exception concerns an Impact-5 risk (Trust Board escalation threshold per [../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology](../../04-Risk-Management/047-risk_methodology.md) §4.2).
