# 1. Introduction

Westbridge University Hospitals Foundation Trust (WUHNFT) is a fictional NHS healthcare organisation created for portfolio purposes. The Trust represents a large, digitally enabled healthcare provider operating within the NHS environment and serving communities across the West Midlands region.

The Trust delivers a wide range of acute, specialist, diagnostic, and community healthcare services. As a modern healthcare provider, WUHNFT relies heavily on digital technology to support clinical decision-making, improve patient outcomes, enable efficient service delivery, and maintain continuity of care.

The increasing dependence on digital systems has created a complex cyber security landscape. Clinical systems, medical devices, cloud services, third-party suppliers, and patient-facing platforms must operate securely while remaining available to healthcare professionals when needed.

Following increasing concerns about cyber threats affecting healthcare organisations nationally, the Trust Board has commissioned **Project SentinelCare** to assess and improve its cyber security governance, risk management, and compliance capabilities.

# 2. Organisation Overview

Westbridge University Hospitals Foundation Trust provides acute and specialist healthcare services across the West Midlands region.

The Trust operates a combination of hospital-based and community healthcare facilities, supporting both routine and emergency healthcare delivery.

The organisation operates:

- Two acute hospital sites
- Three community healthcare centres
- Specialist cancer treatment services
- Emergency department services
- Diagnostic imaging departments
- Pathology laboratories
- Pharmacy services
- Research and clinical trial facilities

The Trust provides healthcare services to approximately:

| Service Area | Annual Volume |
|---|---:|
| Patients Served | 850,000 |
| Outpatient Appointments | 2.5 million |
| Emergency Admissions | 180,000 |

Due to the scale and complexity of its operations, maintaining secure and resilient digital services is critical to patient safety and operational continuity.

# 3. Mission and Strategic Purpose

The Trust's mission is to provide safe, effective, and compassionate healthcare supported by secure and reliable digital services.

WUHNFT aims to:

- Deliver high-quality patient care.
- Improve healthcare outcomes through digital innovation.
- Enable secure information sharing across healthcare providers.
- Protect confidential patient and staff information.
- Maintain resilient clinical services.
- Support research and healthcare innovation.

Cyber security is recognised as an essential enabler of the Trust's strategic objectives rather than solely an information technology responsibility.

# 6. Digital Transformation Journey

Over the past five years, WUHNFT has undergone significant digital transformation to improve healthcare delivery and patient experience.

Key digital initiatives include:

- Implementation of Electronic Patient Record (EPR) capabilities.
- Expansion of patient-facing digital services.
- Increased adoption of cloud technologies.
- Integration of healthcare systems.
- Introduction of remote consultation services.
- Improved data analytics capabilities.

While digital transformation has improved efficiency and accessibility, it has also increased the Trust's cyber security responsibilities.

The expanded digital estate has introduced additional risks including:

- Increased attack surface.
- Greater dependency on third-party suppliers.
- More complex identity and access requirements.
- Increased volumes of sensitive healthcare data.
- Greater dependency on system availability.

# 10. Cyber Security Context

Healthcare organisations are increasingly targeted by cyber threats due to the value of health information and the critical nature of healthcare services.

The Trust faces several cyber security challenges:

- Phishing and credential theft.
- Ransomware threats.
- Legacy medical technology.
- Supplier security risks.
- Excessive access privileges.
- Data governance challenges.
- Limited security visibility.

These challenges have driven the requirement for Project SentinelCare.

# 11. Project SentinelCare Context

Project SentinelCare has been commissioned to improve the Trust's cyber security maturity by establishing stronger governance, risk management, and compliance processes.

The programme will support the Trust in:

- Understanding its cyber risk exposure.
- Improving regulatory compliance.
- Strengthening security governance.
- Improving incident preparedness.
- Enhancing executive reporting.
- Building a culture of security awareness.

The expected outcome is a more resilient healthcare organisation capable of protecting patients, clinical services, and sensitive information against evolving cyber threats.
