# Records Retention Schedule

**Organisation:** Westbridge Hospitals Trust (WHT)  
**Document Type:** Records Retention Schedule  
**Owner:** Records Management Team / Data Protection Officer (DPO)  
**Classification:** Portfolio Case Study – Fictional Organisation  
**Version:** 1.0  



# 1. Purpose

The stakeholder register ([../01-Discovery/006-stakeholders](../01-Discovery/006-stakeholders.md) §5) names a Records Management Team responsible for "appropriate retention and disposal of records," but until this document no retention schedule existed anywhere in the programme. This schedule closes that gap: it defines how long each category of Trust information is retained, the legal or regulatory basis for that period, and how it is disposed of once the retention period expires.



# 2. Scope

This schedule applies to all record categories described in [061-data_classification](061-data_classification.md) §9, across clinical, corporate, and digital systems. It does not cover records governed under separate statutory retention regimes outside the Trust's direct control (e.g. clinical trial data retained under sponsor-specific requirements), which are excluded per [../01-Discovery/005-project_scope](../01-Discovery/005-project_scope.md).



# 3. Retention Principles

Retention periods are set in line with:

- The UK GDPR **storage limitation principle** (Article 5(1)(e)) — personal data must not be kept longer than necessary for the purpose it was collected for.
- The **NHS Records Management Code of Practice**, which sets minimum retention periods for health and corporate records.
- Statutory retention requirements under the Public Records Act, Limitation Act 1980, and sector-specific NHS guidance.

Where a record contains multiple data categories with different retention requirements, the **longest applicable period** governs, unless the record can be reasonably decomposed.



# 4. Retention Schedule

| Record / Data Category | Classification | Retention Period | Legal / Regulatory Basis | Disposal Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Adult health records (EPR) | Restricted | 8 years after last treatment, or until 25th birthday if patient was a minor at last treatment | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion, per [061-data_classification](061-data_classification.md) §10 |
| Maternity records | Restricted | 25 years after birth | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion |
| Mental health records | Restricted | 20 years after last contact, or 8 years after death | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion |
| Laboratory and diagnostic results | Restricted | 8 years, aligned to the parent health record | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion |
| Medical imaging | Restricted | 8 years, aligned to the parent health record | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion |
| Theatre and procedure records | Restricted | 8 years, aligned to the parent health record | NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Secure deletion |
| Staff records (HR) | Confidential | 6 years after employment ends | Limitation Act 1980 / NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Confidential waste disposal |
| Payroll and financial records | Confidential | 6 years after the financial year they relate to | HMRC requirements | Confidential waste disposal |
| Supplier contracts | Confidential | 6 years after contract expiry | Limitation Act 1980 | Confidential waste disposal |
| Security assessments and audit reports | Confidential | 6 years | Internal governance requirement | Confidential waste disposal |
| Cyber risk register entries (superseded versions) | Confidential | 3 years after supersession | Internal governance requirement, supports audit trail | Secure deletion |
| Internal procedures and operational documents | Internal | Until superseded, plus 2 years | Internal governance requirement | Standard deletion |
| Published reports and public communications | Public | Indefinite (public record) | Public Records Act | Not applicable |



# 5. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Records Management Team | Owns and maintains this schedule; coordinates scheduled disposal activity | 
| Information Asset Owner (IAO) | Confirms retention periods for assets under their ownership, per [061-data_classification](061-data_classification.md) §11 |
| Data Protection Officer | Provides guidance on UK GDPR storage limitation compliance |
| System Owner | Implements technical retention and deletion controls within their system |

Roles are consistent with those defined in [../05-Governance/052-roles_and_responsibilities](../05-Governance/052-roles_and_responsibilities.md).



# 6. Compliance Alignment

This schedule supports:

| Requirement | Alignment |
|---|---|
| UK GDPR | Storage limitation principle (Article 5(1)(e)) |
| NHS Records Management Code of Practice | Minimum and maximum retention periods for health and corporate records |
| NHS DSP Toolkit | Evidences a defined records retention and disposal process |
| ISO/IEC 27001 | Supports information lifecycle management controls |



# 7. Review and Maintenance

This schedule is reviewed annually by the Records Management Team, or sooner following a change to NHS Records Management Code of Practice guidance or applicable regulation. Information Asset Owners are responsible for flagging any record category not yet covered by this schedule.
